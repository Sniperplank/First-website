Experience

My experience with programming

High School

I went to T.L. Kennedy high school and it was in grade 11 when I was introduced to programming. Before that point I had no idea what coding was, or how it worked. Even though I was super interested in computers. I took computer science again in grade 12, and even though my marks were barely getting me to pass the course, because of my lack of understanding of coding, I loved programming.

Self-Teaching

After I finished high school. I took one gap year to work. During that year, I was so interested in my new found hobby that was introduced to me in high school. I was watching a lot of tutorials on how to code and did some small projects. And I learned a lot during that time.

Creating Games

During my time off school, I played a lot of video games. One day, I thought, if I like playing games so much, and I love coding and making stuff. Why don't I try to make my own game. And with that, I started looking into tutorials on how to create my own game. I picked up Unity Game engine and I created a lot of small "games" that had basic movement and very small levels. However, my last game was a game that I was satisfied with. And I will share more about it in the Hobbies page.

College

Currently I go to Humber college and I take the Computer Programming and Analysis program. And so far, I have learned a lot about programming. This is still the very beginning of my journy with programming.
